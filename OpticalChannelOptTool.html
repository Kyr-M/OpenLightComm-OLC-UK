An Optical Channel Launch Power Optimization Tool

In optical multi-band transmission systems, the QoT performance critically depends on the optical channel’s launch power into a fiber-link. A systematic method is needed to deduce the optimal power levels for each and every channel of a multi-band system, to attain either a balanced QoT performance across all bands and/or to tailor the attainable transparent length to high-level decisions from the management layer.

The tool estimates the optimal launch power as a function on the traffic load present in the particular link, the number of optical bands engaged and the high-level policies issued by the management layer like e.g. to collectively maximize the attainable transparent length for all bands (‘flat’ OSNIR performance) or to prioritize the attainable transparent length for few bands at the expense of others (‘step-like’ OSNIR performance)

This tool can be used independently or as part of PCE’s routing engine.
