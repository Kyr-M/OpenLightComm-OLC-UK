Optical Communications Systems Design and Modelling

Physical Layer performance degradations of single and multi-channel propagation in fiber-optic systems sets stringent and complex limits to the attainable “capacity x transparent length” product. The gradual deployment of additional optical bands for transportation sets additional physical layer limitations that are not important in single-optical band systems.

By means of suitable physical phenomena abstractions, based on closed-form mathematical expressions, we have been able to produce models that balance accuracy and computational complexity/speed. These models have merit as stand-alone means to assess the scalability of high-capacity deployments, but can also be integrated with complementary software modules as part of multi-layer optimizations.

Projects: B5G OPEN, Metro-Haul
