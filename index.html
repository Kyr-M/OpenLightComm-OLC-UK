What we do

OpenLightComm (OLC) is an SME founded in 2016 by high caliber professionals and it is now part of the Innovation Martlesham ecosystem at the Adastral Park in Martlesham Heath, Ipswich.

OpenLightComm (OLC)

OLC offers state-of-the-art software products and consulting services in the landscape of the converged fields of classical and quantum Telecommunications networks and Information Technologies.

Our extensive experience and knowledgebase have been acquired by directing and/or conducting research and development for Industry and Academia for over two decades.

OLC is closely associated to OpenLightComm-Europe (OLC-E) which is focusing on EU-related research and development efforts.

Design and Implementation of the QKD Control and Management Layers for Access Network Deployments

The integration of QKD technology for 6G deployments facilitates advanced protection against attacks on critical infrastructures. We detail and demonstrate the Key Management and Control Layer operations of a Free Space Optics-based QKD link for network access. We detail the architectural steps to ensure key delivery and relay functions, showcasing their implementation.

Real-time orchestration of QoS-aware end-to-end slices across a converged Metro and Access network exploiting burst-mode technology

As we enter the B5G/6G era, the converged Access and Metro segments are facing multi-faceted challenges including the heterogeneity of technologies, an unprecedented density and scale for the interconnection patterns, dynamic traffic profiles, and the inefficiency associated with the overprovisioning of dedicated connectivity resources. Here we present a generic, yet comprehensive, architecture that addresses these challenges

SDN-enabled path computation element for autonomous multi-band optical transport networks

This paper reports on the design, implementation, and validation of an SDN control plane for multi-band optical networks with externalized path computation. The SDN control plane relies on extending current open and standard interfaces to support dynamic service management and decoupled path computation services on multi-band optical networks while accounting for physical layer impairments, which is critical for successful service provisioning.
